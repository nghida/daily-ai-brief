Gated Attention for Large Language Models: Non-linearity, Sparsity, and Attention-Sink-Free (Alibaba Qwen Team)

Best Paper Award. Systematically shows that adding a sigmoid gate after scaled-dot-product attention heads yields consistent LLM performance gains across 15B MoE and 1.7B dense models trained on up to 3.5T tokens. Practical impact: Qwen incorporated this directly into production models.

Artificial Hivemind: The Open-Ended Homogeneity of Language Models (University of Washington)

Best Paper Award. Using Infinity-Chat (26,000 real-world queries, 31,000+ human preference annotations), evaluated 70+ LLMs and found an "Artificial Hivemind" effect — models exhibit both intra-model repetition and inter-model homogeneity. Critical finding for enterprise AI diversity and risk.

Source

1000 Layer Networks for Self-Supervised RL: Scaling Depth Can Enable New Goal-Reaching Capabilities

Best Paper Award. Successfully trained an RL agent to 1,000+ layers using contrastive self-supervised learning (no reward signals), triggering dramatic emergent goal-reaching capabilities. Challenges the assumption that deep RL is unstable at extreme depth.

Source

Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling (Datasets & Benchmarks Track Best Paper)

Argues that neural scaling laws emerge from representation superposition — models pack more features into hidden dimensions than they can cleanly separate. Under strong superposition, loss decreases proportionally to model size, matching real LLM behavior. Explains why scaling still works.
