Nvidia GTC 2026 Week Kicks Off with Massive Infrastructure Deals

Nvidia's GTC conference begins March 16 in San Jose, but the company is already making waves. Yesterday Nvidia announced a $2 billion investment in Nebius Group to develop AI data centers, on top of its multi-generational partnership with Meta spanning millions of Blackwell and Rubin GPUs. Nvidia also disclosed plans to invest up to $100 billion in OpenAI progressively, with the first gigawatt of Nvidia Vera Rubin systems deploying in H2 2026. The Rubin platform is in full production and major cloud providers — AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, CoreWeave, and Lambda — will be among the first to deploy Rubin-based products.

Anthropic's Pentagon Lawsuit Draws Industry-Wide Support

More than 30 employees from OpenAI and Google DeepMind filed a statement supporting Anthropic's lawsuit against the U.S. Department of Defense, after the Pentagon labeled Anthropic a supply-chain risk for refusing to allow its technology to be used for mass surveillance or autonomous weapons. Google DeepMind chief scientist Jeff Dean is among the signatories. Meanwhile, Google is quietly expanding its own Pentagon AI work, set to provide AI agents to the DoD's 3-million-person workforce for unclassified tasks — positioning itself to benefit from the OpenAI-Anthropic dispute.

ModelOp Report: Enterprise AI Portfolios Explode, But Value Lags

ModelOp's 2026 AI Governance Benchmark Report (released March 11) reveals a dramatic surge in enterprise AI use cases driven by GenAI and agentic tools, but warns of an "AI value illusion." Most enterprises now connect agentic AI systems to 6–20 external tools, expanding third-party risk. Over two-thirds still rely on manual ROI tracking even for production systems. Notably, adoption of commercial AI governance platforms surged from 14% (2025) to nearly 50% in 2026, signaling that enterprises recognize automation and embedded governance are required to keep pace.

Meta Acquires Moltbook, the Viral AI Bot Social Network

Meta acquired Moltbook, the AI bot social platform that went viral last month, racking up millions of registered bots within days of launch. Moltbook's team will join Meta's superintelligence labs. A Meta spokesperson said Moltbook's approach opens up new ways for AI agents to work for people and businesses. This is Meta's latest move to deepen its AI agent capabilities alongside its massive Nvidia infrastructure buildout.
