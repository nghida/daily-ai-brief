NVIDIA GTC 2026 Keynote: Vera Rubin, AWS Mega-Deal, and the "ChatGPT Moment" for Autonomous Driving

Jensen Huang opened GTC 2026 in San Jose yesterday with a keynote packed with announcements. The headline: an expanded AWS partnership deploying more than one million NVIDIA GPUs — spanning Blackwell, Rubin, and Groq 3 LPUs — across Amazon's global infrastructure. Microsoft disclosed it has deployed hundreds of thousands of liquid-cooled Grace Blackwell GPUs and will be the first hyperscaler to power up the new Vera Rubin NVL72 systems. On the product side, NVIDIA debuted NemoClaw, an enterprise AI agent platform for deploying agents across systems, and DSX Air, a simulation tool that cuts AI factory deployment from months to days. Huang declared "the ChatGPT moment for autonomous driving is here," announcing four new autonomous vehicle partners plus a ride-hailing integration with Uber. Roche revealed a 3,500+ Blackwell GPU deployment for pharma R&D. The Vera Rubin roadmap — with Vera Ultra in H2 2027 and Feynman GPUs in 2028 — sets the infrastructure trajectory for the next cycle. Nvidia also teased N1/N1X laptop CPUs targeting Windows-on-Arm. GTC continues through March 19.

DeepSeek V4 and Tencent Hunyuan Both Confirmed for April Launch

China's two most anticipated model releases are converging on the same timeline. DeepSeek V4, a multimodal model generating text, images, and video with improved coding and long-term memory, is confirmed for April after a lighter "V4 Lite" variant appeared on March 9. DeepSeek has optimized V4 for domestic Huawei and Cambricon chips and partnered with Baidu for search integration. Meanwhile, Tencent's new Hunyuan model — led by former OpenAI researcher Shunyu Yao — is a ~30B parameter model focused on in-context learning and agent usability. Separately, Anthropic accused DeepSeek of using thousands of fraudulent accounts to distill millions of Claude conversations for training data. The broader Chinese landscape remains intensely competitive: Moonshot's Kimi 2.5, Minimax M2.5, Zhipu GLM-5, and ByteDance's viral Seedance 2.0 video model are all active, while the "OpenClaw" agentic framework craze has swept Chinese tech companies from Alibaba Cloud to Tencent Cloud.

AMI Labs Raises $1.03B — Yann LeCun Bets on "World Models" Beyond LLMs

Yann LeCun's departure from Meta is now backed by one of the largest seed rounds in AI history. AMI Labs, his Paris-based startup, raised $1.03B at a $3.5B pre-money valuation from investors including NVIDIA, Temasek, and Bezos Expeditions. The thesis: "world models" that learn from physical environments rather than next-token prediction, representing a fundamentally different approach to AI. The timing coincides with Meta delaying its own next-gen "Avocado" model to May and reportedly exploring a Gemini licensing deal with Google — underscoring the talent and strategic void LeCun's departure has created.

Anthropic–Pentagon Battle Continues as Claude Hits #1 on App Store

The DOD's "supply chain risk" designation of Anthropic — after the company refused to allow mass surveillance or autonomous weapons use — has drawn support from over 30 OpenAI and Google DeepMind employees, including chief scientist Jeff Dean.
