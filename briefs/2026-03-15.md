# AI Brief — March 15, 2026

## Top Stories

### OpenClaw Sweeps China as AI Agent Fever Hits New Heights
Nearly 1,000 people lined up outside Tencent's headquarters in Shenzhen for the viral OpenClaw AI agent, and every major Chinese cloud provider has shipped its own version within weeks. The craze underscores how consumer-facing AI agents are now a mainstream battleground in China — though government agencies have already warned against installing OpenClaw on work devices, citing security risks. Meanwhile, Moonshot's Kimi 2.5, MiniMax's M2.5, and Zhipu's GLM-5 all received major updates, keeping the Chinese open-source model race at full speed.

### Zoom Expands Agentic AI Platform for Enterprise Workflow Automation
On March 10, Zoom Communications launched a significant expansion of its enterprise agentic AI platform, embedding workflow orchestration directly into Zoom Workplace, Phone, and CX. The update introduces no-code custom AI agents, enterprise search connectors, and new productivity tools (AI Docs, AI Sheets, AI Slides). This signals a broader industry shift: collaboration platforms are evolving from communication tools into agentic workflow engines.

### Galileo Launches Open-Source "Agent Control" Governance Layer
On March 13, Galileo released Agent Control — an open-source governance framework that lets enterprises define, enforce, and audit conduct rules for AI agents from a single management platform. As companies deploy fleets of autonomous agents, governance infrastructure is becoming as critical as the agents themselves. This addresses the trust gap that has stalled many enterprise agentic deployments.

### AI Funding Shatters Records — $189B in February Alone
Global venture investment hit $189 billion in February, the largest single month on record, driven by OpenAI's historic $110B round (at $840B valuation, led by Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank) and Anthropic's $30B Series G (at $380B valuation). This week, Replit raised $400M at $9B, Nscale secured $2B, and robotics startup Rhoda AI emerged from stealth with $450M. The capital surge reflects investor conviction that AI infrastructure and applications are entering their deployment phase.

## What to Watch

The "agent washing" problem flagged by Deloitte deserves close attention. As every vendor rebrands their automation as "agentic AI," enterprise buyers need rigorous criteria to distinguish genuine multi-step, autonomous agent systems from dressed-up RPA. At the same time, error propagation in agent chains — where one bad output poisons every downstream step — is emerging as the core reliability challenge. Companies investing in agent observability, governance (like Galileo's Agent Control), and validation infrastructure will likely outperform those chasing flashy agent demos without the operational backbone to support them. The agentic AI market may be heading to $139B by 2034, but the winners will be those who solve trust and reliability first.
