## Top Stories

**Micron Forecasts Record Revenue as AI Memory Demand Explodes**

Micron delivered a blowout earnings report, posting $23.86 billion in Q2 revenue and guiding above consensus for Q3 — driven almost entirely by surging demand for high-bandwidth memory powering AI training and inference clusters. The company raised 2026 capital expenditure by $5 billion to over $25 billion, signaling that AI infrastructure buildout is accelerating faster than even bullish forecasts predicted. The stock jumped in after-hours trading, reinforcing the narrative that the AI hardware supply chain remains capacity-constrained heading into H2 2026.

**OpenAI Rolls Out GPT-5.4 Mini Across ChatGPT Tiers**

OpenAI began rolling out GPT-5.4 Mini to Free and Go users via the Thinking feature, with Enterprise customers gaining the option to default Auto routing to the new model. GPT-5 Thinking Mini will be retired in 30 days. The release continues OpenAI's strategy of rapidly cascading capabilities down its model lineup — putting near-frontier reasoning into the hands of free-tier users while nudging enterprise customers toward consumption-based pricing. OpenAI's annualized revenue now exceeds $25 billion, with early IPO preparations reportedly underway for late 2026.

**MiniMax Ships M2.7 — An Agent-Native Model for Enterprise Workflows**

Chinese AI lab MiniMax publicly released M2.7, its latest model optimized for complex agentic workflows in software engineering, office productivity, and research. Available via the MiniMax Agent platform and API, M2.7 joins an increasingly competitive cohort of Chinese models — including Alibaba's Qwen, Baidu's Ernie 5.0, and ByteDance's Seedance 2.0 — that are closing the gap with Western frontier labs. MiniMax's earlier M2.5 model had already been flagged by UBS as the most promising among China's recent wave of releases, and M2.7 extends that positioning.

**Gartner: Worldwide AI Spending to Hit $2.52 Trillion in 2026**

Gartner's latest forecast projects global AI spending will reach $2.52 trillion in 2026, a 44% year-over-year increase. The firm also predicts 40% of enterprise applications will integrate task-specific AI agents by year-end — up from under 5% in 2025. Separately, Gartner's new Data & Analytics predictions warn that GenAI-induced atrophy of critical-thinking skills will push 50% of global organizations to require AI-free skills assessments, highlighting a growing tension between automation velocity and workforce capability.

## Enterprise AI Updates

- **Microsoft Power Platform ships agentic apps.** The March 2026 update introduces autonomous business applications that reason, decide, and adapt — moving beyond traditional Power Automate flows to agent-compatible architectures with built-in governance controls.
- **Google rolls out Gemini upgrades across Workspace.** New Gemini features in Docs, Sheets, Slides, and Drive entered beta today for AI Ultra and Pro subscribers, deepening Google's push to embed AI across the productivity stack.
- **Enterprise AI pricing models are in flux.** At Enterprise Connect 2026 in Las Vegas, the dominant theme was the obsolescence of per-seat pricing in an agentic era. Microsoft previewed Microsoft 365 E7 and Microsoft Agent 365, both GA in May, positioning them as the productivity suite for "human-led, agent-operated" enterprises.
- **Anthropic publishes large-scale public sentiment study.** A study of 80,508 global participants revealed that users' top desires center on professional excellence and life improvement, while primary concerns focus on AI unreliability and job displacement — data points that should inform enterprise change-management strategies.
- **Alibaba's Qwen lead departs amid open-source tensions.** Lin Junyang, technical lead of Alibaba's Qwen model family, resigned on March 3, exposing internal friction between open-source community commitments and commercial monetization pressure. Qwen has overtaken Meta's Llama in cumulative Hugging Face downloads.

## What to Watch

The AI investment cycle is entering a phase where infrastructure spend is no longer speculative — it is reactive to real demand. Micron's $5B capex increase, NVIDIA's $1T chip demand projection from GTC, and Gartner's $2.5T spending forecast all converge on the same signal: the bottleneck is shifting from model capability to physical capacity. For enterprise leaders, the strategic question is shifting from "should we adopt AI" to "can our infrastructure, governance, and pricing models keep pace with consumption." The rapid deprecation cycle (GPT-5 Thinking Mini retiring in 30 days) underscores how fast the floor is rising — budget planning and vendor strategy need to account for quarterly model turnover as the new normal.
