# Nghi's Daily AI Brief — March 14, 2026

## Top Stories

### Meta Delays "Avocado" AI Model, Reportedly Considers Licensing Google Gemini
Meta's next-generation AI model, code-named Avocado, has been pushed from its planned March launch to May. While Avocado reportedly outperforms Meta's previous models and Google Gemini 2.5, it still trails Gemini 3.0 and the latest models from OpenAI and Anthropic. In a surprising twist, Fortune reports that Meta leadership has discussed temporarily licensing Gemini from Google to bridge the gap — a remarkable concession from a company that has long championed open-source AI independence. This comes as Meta simultaneously details four generations of its custom MTIA chips, with HBM bandwidth up 4.5x and FLOPS up 25x from MTIA 300 to MTIA 500.

### NVIDIA GTC 2026 Set to Open Monday with Rubin Ultra and Feynman Teasers
All eyes turn to San Jose as NVIDIA GTC 2026 opens March 16, with Jensen Huang's keynote expected to spotlight the Rubin Ultra — a 144-GPU, 1.5PB/s scale-up architecture — and offer the first details on the next-generation Feynman platform (rumored at 1.6nm with backside power delivery). Ahead of the event, NVIDIA announced $2B investments in both Nebius and Mira Murati's Thinking Machines startup, plus a multigenerational infrastructure partnership with Meta spanning millions of Blackwell and Rubin GPUs. Rubin is in full production, with AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft, and CoreWeave among the first deployers in H2 2026.

### Anthropic vs. Pentagon: OpenAI and Google Engineers Back Anthropic's Lawsuit
In a rare show of cross-company solidarity, more than 30 employees from OpenAI and Google DeepMind — including Google Chief Scientist Jeff Dean — filed a public statement supporting Anthropic's lawsuit against the U.S. Department of Defense. The dispute stems from the Pentagon labeling Anthropic a "supply-chain risk" after the AI firm refused to allow its technology to be used for mass surveillance of Americans or autonomous weapons targeting. The case is shaping up as a landmark confrontation between frontier AI safety principles and national security applications, with significant implications for how government agencies can procure and deploy advanced AI systems.

### Microsoft E7 "Frontier Suite" Resets Enterprise AI Pricing
Microsoft unveiled its $99-per-user Microsoft 365 E7 "Frontier Suite," arriving May 1, bundling productivity, agent governance, and security into a single premium tier. With only 15 million paid Copilot seats against 450 million commercial Microsoft 365 users (3.3% conversion), there's massive headroom for upsell. Meanwhile, Salesforce countered with its Agentic Enterprise License Agreement (AELA) — an "all you can eat" model for Agentforce — signaling that enterprise AI licensing is rapidly evolving beyond per-token and per-seat models.

## Enterprise AI Updates

- **OpenAI surpasses $25B in annualized revenue** and is reportedly exploring early steps toward a public listing as soon as late 2026. Rival Anthropic is approaching $19B ARR, after rolling out memory features to all Claude users and launching a 1-million-token context window in beta.
- **DeepSeek V4 imminent:** The Hangzhou-based lab is preparing to release its long-awaited multimodal model with 1 trillion parameters — capable of processing text, images, and video. Anthropic separately accused DeepSeek of using thousands of fraudulent accounts to scrape millions of Claude conversations for training data, adding a new front to US-China AI competition.
- **Zoom expanded its agentic AI platform** (March 10) with cross-system workflow orchestration, no-code agent builders, and new AI Docs/Sheets/Slides products embedded in Zoom Workplace and CX.
- **Amazon announced ~16,000 corporate layoffs** citing a strategic shift toward AI-driven automation and agentic workflows, primarily impacting middle management and administrative roles.
- **Enterprise adoption scaling:** McKinsey data shows 88% of organizations now use AI in at least one function, but fewer than 40% have moved beyond pilots. Gartner projects global gen AI spend will hit $2.5B in 2026 — a 4x increase over 2025 — as budget authority shifts from innovation teams to centralized IT.

## What to Watch

NVIDIA GTC 2026 on Monday will likely be the week's defining event for enterprise AI leaders — watch for Rubin Ultra inference cost benchmarks (NVIDIA claims up to 10x lower cost per token vs. Blackwell), which could fundamentally reshape production AI ROI calculations. But the Anthropic–Pentagon dispute may prove equally consequential in the longer arc: if the courts side with Anthropic, it could establish a meaningful legal boundary on government AI deployments, affecting every major enterprise with federal contracts. At the same time, DeepSeek V4's release — combined with Alibaba's Qwen family already overtaking Meta's Llama in global downloads — signals that the US-China AI gap is narrower than official narratives suggest.
