## Top Stories

**NVIDIA GTC 2026 Day 2: Vera Rubin Platform Details, Nemotron 3 Super, and $1T Infrastructure Forecast**
GTC 2026 continued in San Jose with deeper technical reveals. NVIDIA detailed the full Vera Rubin platform — its first vertically integrated AI system built for agentic workloads, combining six new chips (Vera CPU, Rubin GPU, NVLink 6 Switch, ConnectX-9 SuperNIC, BlueField-4 DPU, Spectrum-6 Ethernet Switch) into what Jensen Huang called an "AI factory in a box." The Vera CPU delivers 2x efficiency and 50% faster performance than traditional CPUs. NVIDIA also launched Nemotron 3 Super, a 120B-parameter open model with 12B active parameters designed for complex agentic systems at scale. Huang projected AI chip demand could cross $1 trillion by 2027, doubling earlier estimates. Cloud deployments are accelerating: Google Cloud previewed fractional G4 VMs using NVIDIA vGPU technology, and Meta announced a multiyear strategic partnership spanning on-prem, cloud, and AI infrastructure with millions of Blackwell and Rubin GPUs planned.

**Alibaba's Qwen Team in Crisis as Key AI Talent Exits**
Alibaba's open-source AI efforts are under strain. Technical lead Lin Junyang — the driving force behind the Qwen model family — announced his resignation on March 3, joining two other senior Qwen executives who departed in 2026: Hui Binyuan (head of Qwen Code, left for Meta in January) and Yu Bowen (post-training research). The exodus exposes tensions between Alibaba's open-source ambitions and its push to commercialize flagship models. Despite the turmoil, Alibaba released Qwen3-Max-Thinking, claiming it outperforms major U.S. models. Meanwhile, Baidu's Ernie 5.0 release pushed its Hong Kong-traded shares to a three-year high, with the company claiming it outperforms Google's Gemini-2.5-Pro.

**Gartner: Worldwide AI Spending to Hit $2.52 Trillion in 2026**
Gartner's latest forecast projects global AI spending of $2.52 trillion in 2026, a 44% year-over-year increase, with a trajectory toward $3.3 trillion in 2027. AI infrastructure captures the largest share of investment, nearly 50% of the total uplift. Notably, Gartner warns that AI is in the "Trough of Disillusionment" throughout 2026, meaning enterprises will most often acquire AI through incumbent software providers rather than new moonshot projects. This aligns with McKinsey's finding that while 71% of organizations regularly use gen AI, over 80% report no measurable impact on enterprise EBIT.

Fractal announced the launch of LLM Studio, an enterprise platform for building and running custom language models, demonstrated live at GTC 2026. Built on NVIDIA NeMo and NVIDIA NIM Microservices, the platform targets enterprises seeking to deploy domain-specific models without building infrastructure from scratch.

## Enterprise AI Updates

- **Agentic licensing takes shape:** "All-you-can-eat" Agentic Enterprise License Agreements (ALEAs) are becoming the norm, with Salesforce's Agentforce leading the model. Adobe has shifted to "Generative Credits" while HubSpot's credit-based model drove 20% YoY revenue growth.

- **Gartner: 40% of enterprise apps will embed task-specific AI agents by end of 2026**, up from less than 5% previously. McKinsey reports 23% of organizations are already scaling agentic AI in production, with another 39% experimenting.

- **NVIDIA Agent Toolkit and LangChain partnership:** NVIDIA's open-source Agent Toolkit with OpenShell runtime tops DeepResearch Bench accuracy leaderboards. Adobe, Atlassian, SAP, Salesforce, and ServiceNow are building enterprise agents on this stack.

- **Galileo launches Agent Control:** An open-source governance layer for AI agents establishing universal conduct standards, addressing a critical gap as enterprises scale multi-agent deployments.

- **Data tolls emerge as scaling bottleneck:** Connection fees for agent-to-agent communication are becoming the new cloud egress costs — potentially the biggest hidden risk to scaling agentic AI in 2026.

## What to Watch

The convergence of GTC 2026 announcements and Gartner's spending data paints a clear picture: 2026 is the year AI infrastructure investment hits escape velocity, but enterprise ROI remains elusive. The $2.52T spending forecast alongside the "Trough of Disillusionment" framing means Directors of Enterprise AI face a paradox — budgets are growing but scrutiny on measurable outcomes is intensifying. The winners will be organizations that treat the "agent engine" as a replaceable component and invest instead in proprietary data, governance, and workflow integration. Watch the ALEA pricing evolution closely — it will determine whether agentic AI becomes a sustainable business model or another SaaS upsell cycle.
