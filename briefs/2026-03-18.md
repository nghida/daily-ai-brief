## Top Stories

**NVIDIA GTC 2026: Vera Rubin Platform, Groq Acquisition Pays Off, and a $1 Trillion Chip Forecast**

NVIDIA's GTC 2026 keynote in San Jose dominated the news cycle this week. CEO Jensen Huang unveiled the Vera Rubin platform — six codesigned chips spanning CPU, GPU, networking, and DPU — engineered to slash training time and inference costs for the next generation of AI workloads. Rubin-based systems will ship in H2 2026, with AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, and Oracle among the first deployers. Huang also debuted the Groq 3 LPU, the first product from NVIDIA's $20B acquisition of inference chip startup Groq, shipping in Q3. The biggest headline: Huang projects $1 trillion in AI chip purchase orders through 2027, doubling his estimate from last year.

**Zoom Expands Enterprise Agentic AI Platform Across Collaboration Stack**

Zoom Communications announced a major expansion of its agentic AI platform, embedding workflow orchestration directly into Zoom Workplace, Phone, and CX products. Organizations can now deploy prebuilt AI agents for Sales, IT, and Marketing — or build custom agents with no code. The move positions Zoom alongside Salesforce, Microsoft, and ServiceNow in the race to make agentic AI a default layer in enterprise collaboration, not a standalone product.

**A16z Closes $15 Billion Mega-Fund, Its Largest Ever**

Andreessen Horowitz closed $15 billion in new funds — its largest fundraise to date — with co-founder Ben Horowitz framing the capital as a bet on AI and crypto infrastructure that advances American competitiveness. A16z was the second most active post-seed VC globally in 2025 with 165+ deals, backing Cursor-maker Anysphere, Harvey, ElevenLabs, and Databricks. Fresh March deals include Lio AI ($30M Series A for AI procurement), Nexthop AI ($500M Series B for AI networking), and Mind Robotics ($500M Series A for industrial robotics).

**Nebius Raises $3.75B for AI Cloud Infrastructure**

AI cloud platform Nebius announced a $3.75 billion capital raise through convertible notes and loans, following a strategic partnership with NVIDIA that includes a $2B NVIDIA investment. The raise underscores growing investor appetite for dedicated AI inference and training infrastructure outside the hyperscalers.

## Enterprise AI Updates

- **Consumption-based AI pricing is reshaping SaaS budgets.** Salesforce raised prices across enterprise offerings by ~6%, hiked Slack business plans 20%, and introduced new Agentforce pricing. Forrester warns AI spend risks becoming as unpredictable as runaway cloud costs.
- **100% of surveyed enterprises plan to expand agentic AI in 2026,** per CrewAI's annual survey. 65% already use AI agents in production, and 81% say adoption is scaled or actively expanding across teams.
- **Shadow AI is outpacing IT governance.** ChatGPT is now the #1 most-expensed app by transaction count, 16% of the top 50 expensed apps are AI-native, and 83% of enterprises report Shadow AI growing faster than IT can track.
- **GitHub Agentic Workflows entered technical preview,** letting developers define automation goals in Markdown files — enabling LLM-driven issue triage, doc updates, CI troubleshooting, and test improvements directly in repositories.
- **DeepSeek V4 and Tencent Hunyuan confirmed for April launch.** DeepSeek's multimodal trillion-parameter model is being optimized for Huawei and Cambricon domestic chips, while Tencent's Hunyuan — led by former OpenAI researcher Shunyu Yao — targets agent usability over benchmark chasing.

## What to Watch

The convergence of GTC announcements and record VC activity signals that AI infrastructure is entering its buildout phase at industrial scale. NVIDIA's Vera Rubin roadmap, Meta's multiyear GPU partnership, and Nebius's $3.75B raise all point to inference capacity as the next bottleneck — and the next investment category. For enterprise leaders, the immediate question is whether your AI workload architecture is ready for consumption-based economics, or whether "shadow AI" spend will force that conversation first.
