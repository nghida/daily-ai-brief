## Top Stories

**Microsoft Unveils $99/mo E7 Tier — AI Becomes Mandatory in Enterprise Licensing**
Microsoft announced Microsoft 365 E7 on March 9, generally available May 1. At $99/user/month (up from $60 for E5), E7 bundles Copilot, Entra identity tools, and the new Agent 365 product for managing enterprise AI agents. Microsoft also introduced Copilot Cowork — built in partnership with Anthropic — for multi-step autonomous tasks like scheduling emails and meeting prep. Info-Tech Research Group warns the cumulative effect of Microsoft's 2025–2026 pricing changes could impose a 25% cost increase on typical $10M Enterprise Agreements. CIOs should model renewal impact now.
[Source](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/09/microsoft-office-365-e7-copilot-ai.html) | [Source](https://www.geekwire.com/2026/microsofts-new-copilot-cowork-integrates-anthropics-claude-in-rollout-of-new-e7-licensing-tier/)

**Anthropic-Pentagon Battle Escalates — Rival Lab Employees Rally in Support**
Over 30 employees from OpenAI and Google DeepMind — including DeepMind chief scientist Jeff Dean — filed a legal brief supporting Anthropic's lawsuit against the Department of Defense. The Pentagon designated Anthropic a "supply chain risk" after the company refused to permit mass surveillance or autonomous weapons use. Google is deepening its own Pentagon AI engagement to fill the gap. Anthropic's $200M DOD contract and hundreds of millions more in federal revenue hang in the balance.
[Source](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/openai-and-google-employees-rush-to-anthropics-defense-in-dod-lawsuit/) | [Source](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/google-deepens-pentagon-ai-push-after-anthropic-sues-trump-admin.html)

**Gartner: Global AI Spending to Hit $2.52 Trillion in 2026**
Gartner projects worldwide AI spending will reach $2.52 trillion in 2026 — a 44% YoY increase — with $1.37T allocated to infrastructure alone. However, over 80% of organizations report no measurable EBIT impact from gen AI despite 71% regularly using it. The gap between investment and ROI remains the central challenge for enterprise AI leaders.
[Source](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2026-1-15-gartner-says-worldwide-ai-spending-will-total-2-point-5-trillion-dollars-in-2026)

**Nvidia GTC 2026 Begins March 16 — Rubin Platform in Focus**
Nvidia's GTC conference starts next week in San Jose (March 16–19). The spotlight is on the Rubin platform — six new chips including the Vera CPU and Rubin GPU delivering 50 PF NVFP4 inference per chip, a 5x jump over Blackwell GB200 NVL72. AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, and OCI will deploy Rubin-based instances in H2 2026. Nvidia also formalized a multiyear partnership with Meta spanning millions of Blackwell and Rubin GPUs. Separately, NemoClaw — Nvidia's open-source AI agent platform for enterprises — is expected to be unveiled at GTC.
[Source](https://nvidianews.nvidia.com/news/rubin-platform-ai-supercomputer) | [Source](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/nvidia-open-source-ai-agent-platform-nemoclaw-wired-agentic-tools-openclaw-clawdbot-moltbot.html)
