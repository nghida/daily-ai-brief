## 📚 Academic Papers Worth Reading

### NeurIPS 2025 — Best Papers (Awarded November 2025)

**Gated Attention for Large Language Models: Non-linearity, Sparsity, and Attention-Sink-Free** *(Alibaba Qwen Team)*
Best Paper Award. Systematically shows that adding a sigmoid gate after scaled-dot-product attention heads yields consistent LLM performance gains across 15B MoE and 1.7B dense models trained on up to 3.5T tokens. Practical impact: Qwen incorporated this directly into production models.
[Source](https://www.alizila.com/alibaba-qwen-wins-neurips-2025-best-paper-award-for-breakthrough-in-attention-mechanisms/)

**Artificial Hivemind: The Open-Ended Homogeneity of Language Models** *(University of Washington)*
Best Paper Award. Using Infinity-Chat (26,000 real-world queries, 31,000+ human preference annotations), evaluated 70+ LLMs and found an "Artificial Hivemind" effect — models exhibit both intra-model repetition and inter-model homogeneity. Critical finding for enterprise AI diversity and risk.
[Source](https://news.cs.washington.edu/2026/01/22/allen-school-researchers-earn-neurips-best-paper-award-for-artificial-hivemind-effect-across-llm-open-ended-generation/)

**1000 Layer Networks for Self-Supervised RL: Scaling Depth Can Enable New Goal-Reaching Capabilities**
Best Paper Award. Successfully trained an RL agent to 1,000+ layers using contrastive self-supervised learning (no reward signals), triggering dramatic emergent goal-reaching capabilities. Challenges the assumption that deep RL is unstable at extreme depth.
[Source](https://blog.neurips.cc/2025/11/26/announcing-the-neurips-2025-best-paper-awards/)

**Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling** *(Datasets & Benchmarks Track Best Paper)*
Argues that neural scaling laws emerge from representation superposition — models pack more features into hidden dimensions than they can cleanly separate. Under strong superposition, loss decreases proportionally to model size, matching real LLM behavior. Explains why scaling still works.
[Source](https://blog.neurips.cc/2025/11/26/announcing-the-neurips-2025-best-paper-awards/)

### ICLR 2026 — Notable Oral Papers (Conference: April 23–27, Rio de Janeiro)
*5,300+ accepted papers; 223 oral presentations*

**AgentFlow: Trainable In-the-Flow Agentic Reasoning**
A 4-module framework (planner, executor, verifier, generator) with evolving memory that applies outcome-driven RL directly inside the multi-turn agent loop. A 7B-backbone AgentFlow beats GPT-4o with average gains of +14.9% search, +14.0% agentic, +14.5% math, +4.1% scientific tasks. Key result: small open models with the right training loop can exceed frontier proprietary models.
[Source](https://iclr.cc/virtual/2026/papers.html)

**MedAgentGym: Benchmarking LLM Agents for Biomedical Reasoning**
Scalable training and evaluation environment with 72,413 task instances across 129 categories from 12 real-world biomedical scenarios. Closes the gap between academic LLM research and clinical deployment requirements.
[Source](https://iclr.cc/virtual/2026/papers.html)

**FaithCoT-Bench: Instance-Level Chain-of-Thought Faithfulness**
Expert-annotated benchmark with 1,000+ trajectories from four LLMs across four domains, including 300+ unfaithful instances. First comprehensive benchmark for measuring whether a model's stated reasoning actually matches its computation — directly relevant to enterprise AI auditing.
[Source](https://iclr.cc/virtual/2026/papers.html)

**HEX: Hidden Semi-Autoregressive Experts**
Training-free inference method that boosts reasoning accuracy 3.56x on GSM8K (24.72% → 88.10%) and dramatically improves MATH benchmark (16.40% → 40.00%) with no fine-tuning. Applies to existing deployed models — immediate enterprise applicability.
[Source](https://iclr.cc/virtual/2026/papers.html)

**Eliciting Reasoning with Cognitive Tools** *(DAIR.AI highlight)*
Modular tool-based reasoning inspired by cognitive science — LLM calls self-contained "cognitive tools" to scaffold internal reasoning. Significant pass@1 accuracy boost on AIME 2024, MATH500, and AMC across Qwen2.5, Llama3, and GPT-4.1. Points toward modular, auditable reasoning pipelines for enterprise use.
[Source](https://www.dair.ai/)

**AlphaEvolve** *(Google DeepMind — DAIR.AI highlight)*
Coding agent that uses LLM-guided evolution to discover new algorithms: LLMs generate code changes, evaluators provide feedback, an evolutionary loop iteratively improves solutions. Has already rediscovered and improved upon algorithms used in production Google systems.
[Source](https://www.dair.ai/)
