## Top Stories

**ARC-AGI-3 Launches — Frontier AI Scores Under 1% While Humans Hit 100%**

The AI Futures Project unveiled ARC-AGI-3 on March 25, the most demanding public benchmark to date. Unlike prior ARC iterations, ARC-AGI-3 is fully interactive: it requires goal inference, active exploration, persistent memory, and multi-step planning — with zero instructions provided upfront. Every frontier LLM tested scored below 1%; humans scored 100%. The result landed like a cold splash on the AI community. For enterprise AI leaders, the benchmark underscores a critical gap: current LLMs remain brittle when context cannot be assumed and task structure must be inferred. The benchmark is already shaping roadmaps at Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google DeepMind, where beating it is now a stated internal priority.

**Anthropic Confirms Leaked Reasoning Model — Describes It as "Step Change"**

Anthropic broke its silence on a leaked model that had circulated in developer communities this week, confirming the model's existence and describing it as representing a meaningful step forward in reasoning capability. Rather than denying the leak, Anthropic's response strongly suggests an imminent official release. Separately, the company announced AutoDream for Claude Code — a background sub-agent that automatically consolidates, prunes, and reorganizes memory files across sessions to reduce context bloat. Both developments signal Anthropic is accelerating on two fronts simultaneously: frontier reasoning capability and developer tooling usability.

**OpenAI's "Spud" Completes Development; IPO Preparations Begin**

OpenAI's internally codenamed next flagship model "Spud" has completed initial development as of late March 2026, with a public launch expected within weeks. Simultaneously, OpenAI has surpassed $25 billion in annualized revenue — a milestone that has triggered early-stage preparations for a potential public listing as soon as late 2026. SoftBank is reported to be assembling a $40 billion commitment to the company. For enterprise buyers, Spud's release is expected to push GPT-5.4's capabilities further into autonomous multi-step workflow territory, with implications for how AI is priced and licensed at scale.

## What to Watch

ARC-AGI-3 is the benchmark that should be on every enterprise AI leader's radar this month. Its structure — no instructions, active exploration required, memory across steps — is effectively a proxy for real-world agentic deployment conditions. The fact that every frontier model tested scored under 1% is not cause for alarm but rather a useful calibration tool: it maps precisely where autonomous agents break down today. Watch how Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google respond to it in their next model announcements. The labs that close the ARC-AGI-3 gap fastest will likely define the next generation of enterprise-grade agentic capabilities.
