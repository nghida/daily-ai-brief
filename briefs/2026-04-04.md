## Top Stories

**OpenAI's Next Frontier Model — "Spud" Expected Mid-to-Late April**

OpenAI has completed pre-training on its next major model, internally codenamed "Spud," and is targeting a release within weeks — likely mid-to-late April as GPT-5.5 or GPT-6. This follows the March 5 release of GPT-5.4, which set record scores on computer-use benchmarks (OSWorld-Verified, WebArena Verified) and a record 83% on OpenAI's GDPval knowledge-work test. GPT-5.4 mini and nano variants followed on March 17. Meanwhile, Anthropic is internally testing "Claude Mythos," described as a "step change in capabilities," expected to land in Q2 2026. Enterprise buyers evaluating multi-year AI contracts should factor in these imminent releases — pricing structures and capability benchmarks are likely to shift materially within the next 30–60 days.

**DeepSeek V4 and Tencent Hunyuan — China's April Model Wave Arrives**

Two major Chinese models are dropping in April. DeepSeek V4 is a multimodal model (text, image, video) with a focus on long-term memory and improved coding — and critically, it will run entirely on Huawei Ascend chips, marking the first major Chinese frontier model to operate within a fully domestic compute stack. Alibaba, ByteDance, and Tencent have placed bulk orders for Huawei chips to support this shift. Tencent's Hunyuan refresh (~30B parameters) will focus on in-context learning and agent usability. For enterprise procurement evaluating low-cost AI alternatives, the China model landscape is increasingly capable and strategically decoupled from NVIDIA GPU supply chains.

**Enterprise AI Pricing Moves to Outcomes — IFS and HubSpot Lead the Shift**

Two significant pricing model changes emerged this week. IFS announced it is abandoning user-based licensing for enterprise AI, replacing it with asset-based pricing: a utility company managing 400 offshore assets pays per asset, not per the 12,000 users and machines accessing the system. HubSpot is shifting Breeze AI Agents from usage-based credits to outcome-based pricing effective April 14. Both moves reflect an industry-wide reckoning: enterprise buyers are rejecting seat-count models that don't map to AI ROI. For Directors of Enterprise AI negotiating vendor contracts in 2026, outcome-based pricing offers a more defensible internal justification for spend — but requires clearer upfront definition of measurable outcomes.

**Q1 2026 VC Hits $300B — AI Dominates; Infrastructure Remains the Core Bet**

Investors poured $300 billion into 6,000 startups in Q1 2026, up over 150% year-over-year — the highest quarterly startup investment on record — with AI and infrastructure capturing the majority. Big tech has committed staggering capex: Amazon $200B, Microsoft ~$150B annualized, Google $175–185B, and Meta $115–135B. Samsung is leading a $73B+ 2026 AI semiconductor investment plan. Custom inference chips from Amazon (Trainium/Inferentia), Google (TPUs), Groq, and Cerebras are increasingly competitive with NVIDIA in inference workloads — a shift with direct implications for enterprise cloud infrastructure costs through 2027.

## Enterprise AI Updates

- **Glean doubles ARR to $200M** at a $7.2B valuation, driven by its Agentic Engine 2 with adaptive planning, parallel sub-agent orchestration, and a unified model hub supporting 15+ LLMs. Enterprise knowledge-layer AI is clearly a durable category, not a feature.
- **Agentic monitoring now a distinct product category**: Codenotary launched AgentMon this week — watching for data leaks, cost overruns, and security rule violations across deployed agents. As agent deployment scales, governance tooling is no longer optional.
- **SpendHQ acquired Sligo AI** to embed agentic AI into enterprise procurement workflows, signaling that back-office function-specific AI acquisitions are accelerating.
- **Qualys launched Agent Val**, an autonomous security agent that identifies and remediates vulnerable code without human intervention — early evidence of fully autonomous IT operations agents entering production.
- **40% of enterprise applications expected to include task-specific AI agents by end of 2026** (Gartner), up from under 5% in 2025. Portfolio rationalization of existing RPA and workflow automation vendors should be on every enterprise AI roadmap now.

## What to Watch

The competitive window for AI model selection is narrowing rapidly. OpenAI's "Spud" and Anthropic's "Claude Mythos" are both expected within weeks — and if either represents the "step change" their internal descriptions suggest, it will reset the benchmark landscape that enterprise buyers are currently using to make procurement decisions. The more durable strategic question is not which model wins this cycle, but whether enterprises are building infrastructure that is model-agnostic. The companies that will benefit most from the accelerating model release cadence are those that have abstracted model selection behind an orchestration layer — enabling fast model swaps without re-engineering every workflow. Evaluate your current agentic architecture for model portability before locking into provider-specific tooling.
