## Top Stories

**NVIDIA GTC Day 2 — New Inference Chip With Groq Technology, Vera CPU Takes Center Stage**
NVIDIA's GTC 2026 conference (March 16–19) is now underway in San Jose, and the headlines from Jensen Huang's keynote are reshaping the infrastructure conversation. The biggest reveal: a new inference-focused processor incorporating IP from NVIDIA's Groq acquisition, designed for dramatically faster and more efficient AI responses. NVIDIA also spotlighted its next-gen Vera CPU as the critical bottleneck-breaker for agentic AI workloads — a tacit admission that GPUs alone can't power the multi-agent orchestration enterprises are demanding. The Vera Rubin platform's six codesigned chips deliver an estimated 3.3–5x performance improvement over Blackwell. AWS, Google Cloud, Azure, OCI, CoreWeave, Lambda, and Nebius will deploy Vera Rubin instances in H2 2026. For enterprise AI leaders, this week's announcements lock in the infrastructure roadmap through 2028.

**OpenAI Courts Private Equity for $10B Enterprise AI Joint Venture**
OpenAI is in advanced talks with TPG, Advent International, Bain Capital, and Brookfield Asset Management to form an enterprise AI joint venture valued at roughly $10 billion pre-money. The structure would distribute OpenAI's enterprise products across the PE firms' portfolio companies and beyond — giving OpenAI a faster route into corporate adoption while providing PE firms a lifeline for portfolio companies exposed to AI disruption. This follows OpenAI's record $110B raise on February 27 at an $840B post-money valuation, with Amazon committing $50B as exclusive third-party cloud partner. Annualized revenue now exceeds $25B. The PE play signals OpenAI's pivot from model superiority to distribution dominance.

**DeepSeek V4 Imminent — Multimodal, Trillion-Parameter, and Trained on NVIDIA's Latest**
DeepSeek's long-awaited V4 model is expected any day now, bringing 1 trillion parameters and native multimodal capabilities spanning text, image, and video generation. Notably, the model was trained on NVIDIA's most advanced AI chips while also being optimized for Chinese chipmakers Huawei and Cambricon — a dual-stack strategy that hedges against export controls. Alibaba preemptively upgraded its Qwen flagship with enhanced agent capabilities and two-hour video understanding, while Baidu's Ernie 5.0 launch pushed its Hong Kong shares to a three-year high. However, Alibaba's AI bench is thinning: three senior Qwen executives have departed in 2026, including technical lead Lin Junyang, exposing tensions between open-source ambitions and commercialization pressure.

**Nscale Raises $2B Series C at $14.6B Valuation for AI Infrastructure**
London-based AI infrastructure hyperscaler Nscale closed a $2 billion Series C led by Aker and 8090 Industries, marking one of the largest infrastructure rounds of the year. Meanwhile, Advanced Machine Intelligence — the startup co-founded by former Meta chief scientist Yann LeCun — raised $1.03B for "world models" that learn from physical environments. These mega-rounds underscore the capital intensity of the current AI buildout: 17 US-based AI companies have closed $100M+ rounds in just the first six weeks of 2026, with three crossing the billion-dollar mark.

- **Zoom expands agentic AI platform** with new workflow orchestration across Zoom Workplace, Phone, and CX. Prebuilt agents for Sales, IT, and Marketing require no coding — a signal that agentic capabilities are becoming table stakes for collaboration platforms.

- **GitHub launches Agentic Workflows in technical preview**, enabling LLM-powered automation for issue triage, documentation updates, CI troubleshooting, and test improvements — all defined via simple Markdown files within repositories.

- **D-ID ships V4 Expressive Visual Agents** (March 16) — ultra-high-fidelity digital humans for real-time LLM-connected conversations at enterprise scale, targeting customer service and training use cases.

- **"Agent washing" emerges as a real concern**: industry analysts estimate only ~130 of thousands of claimed AI agent vendors are building genuinely agentic systems. Galileo launched Agent Control, an open-source governance layer for establishing universal AI agent behavior standards.

## What to Watch

The convergence of NVIDIA's inference-specific silicon, OpenAI's PE-backed distribution play, and DeepSeek's imminent multimodal release is compressing the competitive cycle to weeks rather than quarters. The real enterprise signal this week isn't any single model or chip — it's the infrastructure layer locking in. With $600B in Big Tech AI capex expected this year and Gartner projecting 40% of enterprise apps will embed task-specific agents by year-end, the window for organizations to define their agentic architecture is narrowing fast. Leaders should be pressure-testing their inference cost models and multi-vendor chip strategies now, before the Vera Rubin and Helios deployments reshape pricing in H2.
