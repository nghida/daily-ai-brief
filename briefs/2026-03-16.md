## Top Stories

**NVIDIA GTC 2026 Kicks Off — Rubin Platform, NemoClaw, and a $2B Nebius Investment**
Jensen Huang delivers the GTC 2026 keynote today at 11 a.m. PT in San Jose. The centerpiece: the Vera Rubin platform — six codesigned chips (Vera CPU, Rubin GPU, NVLink 6 Switch, ConnectX-9 SuperNIC, BlueField-4 DPU, and Spectrum-6 Ethernet Switch) featuring 336 billion transistors and HBM4 memory, delivering an estimated 3.3–5x performance improvement over Blackwell in FP4 inference. AWS, Google Cloud, Microsoft Azure, OCI, CoreWeave, Lambda, and Nebius will deploy Vera Rubin-based instances in H2 2026. NVIDIA also disclosed a $2B investment in Nebius, a multigenerational infrastructure deal with Meta spanning millions of Blackwell and Rubin GPUs, and is expected to unveil NemoClaw — an open-source enterprise AI agent platform. A new inference-focused chip incorporating IP from Nvidia's Groq acquisition is also anticipated. For enterprise AI leaders, GTC sets the infrastructure and agentic AI roadmap for the next 18 months.

**Meta Delays Avocado Model to May, Reportedly in Talks to License Google Gemini**
Meta's next-generation model, code-named Avocado, has been pushed from March to May after internal tests showed it lags behind the latest from OpenAI and Anthropic — particularly in logical reasoning, software development, and agentic behavior. In an unprecedented move, Meta's AI leadership has reportedly discussed temporarily licensing Gemini from Google to power its AI products while Avocado catches up. Meanwhile, former Chief AI Scientist Yann LeCun officially departed Meta and immediately raised $1.03B at a $3.5B pre-money valuation for AMI Labs — a Paris-based startup building "world models" that learn from physical environments rather than next-token prediction. Investors include Nvidia, Temasek, and Bezos Expeditions. The simultaneous talent loss, model delay, and licensing discussions put significant pressure on Meta's AI strategy.

**GPT-5.4 Launches With Native Computer Use — OpenAI's First Desktop-Task Model**
OpenAI released GPT-5.4 on March 5 in three variants: standard, Thinking (reasoning), and Pro (high-performance). It is OpenAI's first general-purpose model with native computer-use capabilities, enabling agents to operate computers and execute multi-step workflows across applications. The API supports context windows up to 1M tokens — the largest from OpenAI. GPT-5.4 scores 83% on OpenAI's GDPval knowledge-work benchmark, is 33% less likely to make individual claim errors, and includes financial plugins for Excel and Google Sheets targeting enterprise finance teams. Pricing: $2.50/$15.00 per 1M tokens (standard), $30/$180 (Pro). OpenAI's annualized revenue now exceeds $25B.

**Anthropic-Pentagon Battle Escalates — Rival Lab Employees Rally in Support**
Over 30 employees from OpenAI and Google DeepMind — including Google chief scientist Jeff Dean — filed an amicus brief supporting Anthropic's lawsuit against the DOD. The Pentagon designated Anthropic a "supply chain risk" after the company refused to permit mass surveillance or autonomous weapons use. Anthropic has now sought an appeals court stay of the designation, arguing it was issued "without observance of the procedures Congress required." Over 100 enterprise customers have contacted Anthropic about the designation, and the company estimates hundreds of millions to billions in potential lost 2026 revenue. Google is deepening its own Pentagon AI engagement to fill the gap.

## 📚 Academic Papers Worth Reading

### Recent ArXiv Highlights (March 2026)

**DIG to Heal: Scaling General-purpose Agent Collaboration via Explainable Dynamic Decision Paths**
Proposes a scalable framework for multi-agent collaboration using dynamic, explainable decision paths. Addresses one of the core challenges in deploying multi-agent systems at enterprise scale: understanding why agents make specific coordination decisions. Directly relevant to teams building agentic workflows with tools like Dify, LangFlow, or OpenManus.
[Source](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

**Try, Check and Retry: A Divide-and-Conquer Framework for Boosting Long-context Tool-Calling Performance of LLMs**
Tackles the reliability gap in LLM tool-calling over long contexts — a critical bottleneck for enterprise agentic AI. The divide-and-conquer approach breaks complex tool-calling chains into verifiable substeps, significantly improving accuracy. Practical implications for production agent deployments using GPT-5.4's new 1M-token context window.
[Source](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

**Ψ0: An Open Foundation Model for Humanoid Loco-manipulation**
Achieves over 40% higher success rates on complex, long-horizon robotic tasks compared to state-of-the-art baselines. A significant step toward general-purpose humanoid robots. As Nvidia's Rubin platform targets robotics workloads, foundation models like Ψ0 indicate what the next wave of embodied AI inference will look like.
[Source](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

### ICLR 2026 — Notable Oral Papers (Conference: April 23–27, Rio de Janeiro)

**AgentFlow: Trainable In-the-Flow Agentic Reasoning**
A 4-module framework (planner, executor, verifier, generator) with evolving memory that applies outcome-driven RL directly inside the multi-turn agent loop. A 7B-backbone AgentFlow beats GPT-4o with +14.9% search, +14.0% agentic, +14.5% math, +4.1% scientific task gains. Key result: small open models with the right training loop can exceed frontier proprietary models.
[Source](https://iclr.cc/virtual/2026/papers.html)

**HEX: Hidden Semi-Autoregressive Experts**
Training-free inference method that boosts reasoning accuracy 3.56x on GSM8K (24.72% → 88.10%) and dramatically improves MATH benchmark (16.40% → 40.00%) with no fine-tuning. Applies to existing deployed models — immediate enterprise applicability for improving reasoning without retraining.
[Source](https://iclr.cc/virtual/2026/papers.html)

**AlphaEvolve** *(Google DeepMind)*
Coding agent using LLM-guided evolution to discover new algorithms: LLMs generate code changes, evaluators provide feedback, an evolutionary loop iteratively improves solutions. Has already rediscovered and improved upon algorithms used in production Google systems. Points toward autonomous algorithm discovery for enterprise optimization.
[Source](https://www.dair.ai/)
